Patrick Kelly — Resume Review

SITUATION
Patrick has not had to use his resume seriously since 2008. He has been applying nonstop
for a few years, is not getting many interviews, and suspects ATS/ranking systems are not
reading or prioritizing the resume well.

WHAT IS WORKING
- Real operational scale: grew Sports Source 2 revenue from under $1M to $4M annually.
- Long tenure and progression at PAINWeek: Program Manager -> Senior Program Director ->
  Director, Multimedia Production.
- Strong hybrid profile: marketing, event operations, digital production, CRM/customer
  support, analytics, and ecommerce.
- Concrete event scale: 50+ events/year, 150-2,500 attendees/exhibitors, 120-page program
  guide, 250+ edited interviews/animations.
- Tool stack is useful: Adobe Creative Suite, Cvent, HubSpot, HelpScout, Asana, video
  editing tools.

MAIN ISSUES HURTING VISIBILITY
1. Headline is too broad. "Marketing/Communications/Event Management" does not tell an
   ATS or recruiter what job family to place him in. He needs targeted versions by role.
2. Summary undersells the strongest proof. The current summary says "strong" and
   "strategic" but does not lead with revenue growth, conference scale, or digital
   campaign ownership.
3. Too many bullets are responsibility-only. More bullets need metrics, scope, frequency,
   stakeholders, budget/vendor/team size, and tools.
4. Formatting likely creates parsing problems. Multiple roles under one employer, tabbed
   spacing, section labels embedded as bullets, and decorative formatting can confuse ATS.
5. Sports Source 2 is underdeveloped. Most current role with the best metric needs more
   context: team size, inventory systems, ecommerce channels, campaign cadence, etc.
6. Skills section is incomplete for current roles. Lists software but not keyword clusters
   recruiters search for.
7. The resume lacks targeting. One all-purpose resume performs poorly against modern ATS
   ranking. Needs base resume plus tailored variants.

RECOMMENDED STRATEGY
Build one clean ATS master resume, then maintain four role-specific versions:
- Marketing Operations / Marketing Specialist
- Event Marketing / Program Manager
- Digital Content / Multimedia Production
- Ecommerce / Marketplace Operations Manager

For every job application, mirror the job description's terms where truthful: "campaign
operations," "event logistics," "CRM," "marketing automation," "stakeholder management,"
"ecommerce marketplace," "vendor coordination," "analytics/reporting," etc.

CHANGES MADE IN THE UPDATED RESUME
- Repositioned Patrick as a marketing, event operations, and ecommerce leader.
- Pulled the $1M -> $4M revenue growth into the top summary.
- Converted responsibility lists into achievement bullets.
- Flattened formatting for ATS readability.
- Consolidated skills into keyword-rich groups.
- Made the PAINWeek career progression readable without burying the promotions.
